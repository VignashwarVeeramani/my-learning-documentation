### What are Stocks and How Does the Market Work?

A **stock** (or share) is a small piece of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder. Companies sell stocks to raise money for growth.

The **stock market** is where these stocks are bought and sold. In Australia, the main market is the **Australian Securities Exchange (ASX)**. Stock prices are driven by supply and demandâ€”if more people want to buy a stock than sell it, the price rises, and vice-versa.

You can make money from stocks in two main ways:
* **Capital Gains:** Selling a stock for more than you paid for it.
* **Dividends:** Companies may distribute a portion of their profits to shareholders.

### Step 1: Get Your Finances in Order ðŸ’°
Before investing, ensure you have:
1.  **A Budget:** Know your income and expenses to see what you can invest.
2.  **An Emergency Fund:** Save 3-6 months of living expenses in an accessible account.
3.  **Managed Debt:** Pay off high-interest debts (like credit cards) first.

### Step 2: Define Your Investing Goals ðŸŽ¯
Ask yourself:
* **Why are you investing?** (e.g., retirement, house deposit)
* **What is your time horizon?** (Long-term investors can often take more risk).
* **What is your risk tolerance?** How comfortable are you with potential short-term losses for long-term gains?

### Step 3: Choose Your Investment Approach
As a beginner, popular options include:
* **Individual Stocks:** Buying shares in specific companies you've researched.
* **Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs):** Funds that hold a basket of different stocks, offering instant diversification. This is highly recommended for beginners.
* **Listed Investment Companies (LICs):** Actively managed funds that trade on the ASX, similar to ETFs.

### Step 4: Open a Brokerage Account ðŸ’»
To trade on the ASX, you need a brokerage account. For beginners, **online brokers** are usually the best choice due to their low fees. When choosing, compare brokerage fees, account fees, and ease of use.

### Step 5: Start Investing ðŸ“ˆ
You don't need a lot of money to begin. A great strategy is **dollar-cost averaging**, where you invest a fixed amount regularly (e.g., monthly). This helps smooth out the effects of market volatility.

### Step 6: Monitor and Review ðŸ“Š
Review your portfolio periodically to ensure it aligns with your goals, but avoid panic-selling during short-term market dips. Investing is a long-term strategy.
